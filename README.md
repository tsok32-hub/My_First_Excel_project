# Green Valley Medical Center — Patient Data Analysis
Analysis of patient records from Green Valley Medical Center to surface operational trends, performance insights, and opportunities for improvement across hospital departments.
# Overview
This project analyzes a dataset of 400 patient records spanning multiple hospital departments. It looks at treatment costs, length of hospital stay, recovery performance, and departmental efficiency to help hospital leadership make data-driven operational decisions.
# Dataset
Records: 400 patients
Scope: Multiple departments
Metrics tracked: treatment cost, hospital stay duration, recovery score, patient volume
# Key Findings
Total treatment cost: $2,020,610.00 across all 400 patients
Average hospital stay: 5.35 days overall; General Medicine had the longest average stay at 5.94 days, suggesting patients in that department require more extended care
Highest average treatment cost: Pediatrics, at $5,587.51 per patient — likely reflecting more complex, intensive treatments
Overall averages: $5,052.53 average treatment cost per patient and 5 days average hospital stay
Highest recovery score: Dermatology, with an average of 76.61 — also the department with the highest patient volume (64 total visits), suggesting a link between treatment effectiveness and patient throughput
# Trends and Interpretation
Hospital stay duration and treatment cost vary substantially by department, with longer stays generally correlating with higher costs — pointing to a relationship between resource utilization and care complexity.
Some departments achieve strong recovery scores despite lower costs and shorter stays, suggesting best practices that could be replicated elsewhere in the hospital.
Patient volume fluctuates across departments, indicating uneven workload distribution.
# Recommendations
Cost efficiency review — Conduct deeper analysis of high-cost departments to identify opportunities for efficiency without compromising care quality.
Staff allocation — Align staffing with patient volume trends to reduce bottlenecks and improve patient flow.
Performance benchmarking — Use high-recovery-score departments as operational models for others.
Stay-duration analysis — Investigate departments with longer average stays for capacity constraints, workflow inefficiencies, or discharge delays.
Predictive analytics — Forecast patient demand to improve resource planning.
Real-time dashboards — Build dashboards to track hospital stay duration, patient volume, treatment cost, and recovery rates on an ongoing basis.
# Conclusion
This analysis surfaces key financial and operational trends that can help hospital leadership improve patient outcomes, increase efficiency, and optimize costs. Targeted operational improvements, guided by data-driven decision-making, can strengthen patient satisfaction and service quality across departments.
